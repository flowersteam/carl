The Artificial Experimentalist: An Autotelic RL Agent that Learns to Guide Self-Organized Phenomena in Artificial Life

Marko Cvjetko1, Benedikt Hartl2, Michael Levin2,3, Clément Moulin-Frier4, Pierre-Yves Oudeyer1

1Inria Centre at the University of Bordeaux · 2Allen Discovery Center, Tufts University · 3Wyss Institute, Harvard University · 4Inria, INSA Lyon, CITI

Submitted to ALife 2026

Abstract

We introduce a closed-loop framework based on autotelic reinforcement learning in which an agent autonomously samples diverse goals and learns a goal-conditioned policy to intervene in a complex system through minimal, local perturbations. We instantiate this framework on Lenia, a continuous cellular automaton, in a system called CARL. CARL discovers stable solitons across a wide range of Lenia update rules, learns to steer the movement direction of existing solitons, and can be used as a real-time interface for humans to guide solitons through maze environments.
